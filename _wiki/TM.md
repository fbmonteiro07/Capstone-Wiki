## Snapshot
Largest global automaker by volume. FY26 consolidated sales (12m to Mar/26) of **9,595k vehicles** (+2.5% YoY) and revenues of **¥50.7tn** (TM FY26Q4 deck, 2026-05-08). Mix by region: N. America 2,934k (largest), Japan 2,082k, Asia incl. China 1,838k, Europe 1,183k, Other 1,659k. "Multi-pathway" electrification strategy: **HEV is the profit pillar** — FY26 electrified retail of 5,040k units (48.1% of the Toyota+Lexus mix), of which HEV 4,620k, PHEV 175k, BEV 243k, FCEV 1k (TM FY26Q4 deck). Beyond autos, it operates Financial Services (consolidated OI ¥709.8bn FY26) and a value-chain business (parts/accessories, used cars, connected) that the company has been highlighting as a margin driver (TM FY26Q4 deck, slide 21). The 20-F frames the sector as "highly volatile" with intensifying competition via CASE (TM 20-F 2026-06-10, risk factors).

## Current state (latest quarter: FY26 actual, reported 2026-05-08)
- **OI FY26 ¥3.766tn (-¥1.029tn YoY), margin 7.4% (from 10.0%)** (TM FY26Q4 deck). Net income attributable ¥3.848tn, margin 7.6%.
- **US tariff is the swing factor.** US tariff impact of **-¥1.38tn on FY26 OI** (TM FY26Q4 deck, slide 7, OI bridge). The company says it "secured profits consistent with guidance" despite this, via volume + price revisions + value chain (slide 3).
- **FX:** average FY26 ¥/US$ **151** (vs 153 in FY25 → -2 ¥, headwind); ¥/€ 175 (from 164, +11 ¥). Total FX effect on OI **-¥195bn** (slide 7). Classic sensitivity: a stronger yen = translational headwind + transactional headwind on Japan exports.
- **Geography (OI ex-swaps, FY26):** N. America **-¥298.6bn (margin -1.4%)**, swung to a loss from a positive ¥104.3bn in FY25 — the epicenter of tariff pain. Japan ¥2.330tn (margin 10.6%, from 14.5%). Asia ¥872.3bn. Europe ¥330.8bn. China (equity method) profit ¥106.9bn (TM FY26Q4 deck, slides 8-9).
- **Guidance FY27 (2026.4-2027.3):** revenues ¥51.0tn (+0.6%), **OI ¥3.0tn (-¥766bn, margin 5.9%)**, NI ¥3.0tn. Volume 9,600k (+0.1%). FX assumption ¥150/US$, ¥180/€ (TM FY26Q4 deck, slide 12). The guided decline comes from a new Middle East impact (-¥400bn), labor/depreciation/R&D, and the deconsolidation of Hino (-¥40bn OI). BEV retail guided to **598k (+146%)** in FY27 — an acceleration, but off a low base (slide 11).
- **Capital return:** FY26 dividend ¥95 (+5), FY27 forecast ¥100 (+5). FY26 brought a **record buyback of ¥3.657tn**, mostly tied to the Toyota Industries take-private via tender offer (shares to be cancelled). FY26 total shareholder return ¥4.895tn (TM FY26Q4 deck, slides 15-16).
- **H1 FY26 (reported 2025-11-05) as a check point:** 1H OI ¥2.006tn (-¥458bn YoY), margin 8.1%; tariff -¥900bn in 1H; volume +5.0% (strong Japan + N.Am). FY26 guidance had been raised from ¥3.2tn→¥3.4tn — i.e., **the current ¥3.766tn actual beat even the upwardly revised guidance** (TM FY26Q2 deck, slides 3,14). FY26 BEV guidance had been cut (308k→277k retail), a sign of BEV demand below plan.

## Debate / thesis
_(Inferred from the 6-K Q&A summaries + 20-F; English-language sell-side coverage non-existent on disk — treat as low conviction.)_
- **Bull:**
  - Leadership in **HEV** at a time when global BEV adoption is decelerating; HEV retail +4.4% FY26 and 4.6M units — structural cash flow while competitors burn on BEV (TM FY26Q4 deck, slide 5).
  - **Resilience of earnings power despite -¥1.38tn of tariff**: FY26 actual beat the company's own revised guidance (TM FY26Q4 vs FY26Q2 decks).
  - **Value-chain** program targeting +¥2.1tn of OI by 2030 (from ¥1.4tn in 2025) and a stated goal of **20% ROE** via business structure reform (TM FY26Q4 deck, slides 21,23).
  - Aggressive shareholder return: rising dividend + record FY26 buyback.
- **Bear:**
  - **US tariff is structural, not a one-off**: N. America already at an operating loss in FY26 (-¥298.6bn) and FY27 guidance cuts OI to ¥3.0tn; new Middle East headwind. Break-even volume "significantly increased over the past two years" — the company's own admission (TM FY26Q2 deck, slide 19).
  - **Secular margin compression**: OI margin 10.0%→7.4%→guidance 5.9%, three years of erosion (TM FY26Q4 deck, slide 29).
  - **BEV below plan** (FY26 guidance cut 308k→277k) — risk of falling behind in markets that adopt BEV faster; the 20-F warns that competing in "electrified vehicles…will be fundamental" with no guarantees (TM 20-F 2026-06-10).
  - **China**: equity-method profit ¥106.9bn FY26, essentially flat; intense local competition (BYD et al.) — a limited-upside thesis.
  - **FX two-way**: FY27 guidance at ¥150/US$ — a yen stronger than that becomes an incremental headwind.
- **Physical AI / humanoid optionality (low signal):** Toyota is *not* one of MS's 20 highlighted humanoid plays and is not framed as a humanoid OEM. The only Toyota datapoint in MS's humanoid map: Toyota is a named co-developer of **OpenVLA**, an open-source vision-language-action robot foundation model built with Stanford, UC Berkeley, Google DeepMind, Physical Intelligence and MIT (OpenX/970k-episode dataset). This is research-consortium participation in physical-AI models, not a productized robotics revenue stream — read it as a credibility marker on the AI/robotics frontier (via the Toyota Research Institute lineage), not a thesis driver (MS Humanoids, 2025).
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** No ratings/PTs in the local filings/transcripts. The one attributable third-party rating now on file: **Morgan Stanley rates Toyota Equal-Weight** (analyst Shinji Kakiuchi), per MS's Global Humanoid coverage list — mkt cap ~$244bn, ADR $18.65 as of 25-Apr-2025; no PT disclosed (MS Humanoids, 2025). ## Risks
- US/Middle East tariffs and trade policy — quantified and recurring headwind (-¥1.38tn FY26; -¥400bn new Middle East in FY27) (TM FY26Q4 deck; 20-F forward-looking factors).
- FX (¥/US$, ¥/€) — transactional exposure (Japan exports) + translational (TM 20-F 2026-06-10).
- CASE / electrification competition — risk of not keeping pace with BEV adoption in fast markets (TM 20-F risk factors).
- China — intense local competition, stagnant equity-method profit.
- Structurally higher break-even volume — less cushion in a volume downturn (TM FY26Q2 deck, slide 19).
- Idiosyncratic item: certification/recall (one-time Hino costs in FY25), reliance on suppliers and raw-material prices (20-F).
