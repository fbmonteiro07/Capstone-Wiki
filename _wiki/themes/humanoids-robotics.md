## What it is / why it matters

The emerging physical-AI hardware category: **humanoid robots** powered by foundation vision-language-action (VLA) models, initially targeting factory/warehouse automation before household deployment. A decade-long horizon but with an extremely steep ramp — 141 models launched since 2024, with industry revenue forecast at ~$5T annually by 2050 (MS). Humanoids are the downstream demand driver for AI silicon (VLA model inference), advanced sensors (6-axis F/T, encoders), and semiconductor manufacturing (actuator tolerances require precision fabrication). The BOM is today 45–48% actuators + 33–35% hands, with the China supply chain dominating at ~1/3 of non-China cost. The read-through for semicap names is years away, but the supply-chain visibility window is opening now.

## Key debates

- **Bull (MS):** $5T annual revenue by 2050; ~50% adoption in upper-middle-income (primarily China), ~30% high-income. ASPs compress from $200K → $50K (high-income) over 25 years; China supply chain already at ~$46K BOM (2025) → $16K by 2034 at 1M units. Integrated OEMs (own brain+body+brand) most valuable; Tesla cited as a potential future leader among integrators (MS, Humanoids, Apr 2025).
- **Bear / long horizon:** Household adoption gated by affordability, safety, and regulatory acceptance — only 80M units in homes by 2050 (92% industrial/commercial). The hardware/AI gap for general-purpose is still a decade away. China precision/lifespan/stability gap vs non-China remains (MS, Humanoids, Apr 2025).
- **Timeline / inflection points:** Decade-plus horizon with a steep ramp once it starts; near-term the actionable signal is supply-chain visibility (actuators, dexterous hands, 6-axis sensors) and the China iteration cadence (Unitree). Semicap read-through is long-dated.
