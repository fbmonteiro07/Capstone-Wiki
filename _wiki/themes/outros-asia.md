## What it is / why it matters

A home for Asia ex-coverage supply-chain themes — the Japanese/Korean/Taiwanese/Chinese component makers that sit upstream of the AI build-out but aren't in the US-centric universe. The opening theme is the **MLCC super-cycle**, framed by Morgan Stanley as "the other AI squeeze": AI is shifting the scarcity story from semiconductors to multilayer ceramic capacitors. A single AI server eats ~440,000 MLCCs (10–15x a traditional server), and producers are reallocating fixed capacity toward high-margin AI parts faster than they can add it — a structural supply-demand mismatch MS likens to the legacy DDR4 DRAM squeeze of two years ago. For a buy-sider the read-through is content/ASP/scarcity value at the Japan-Korea duopoly (Murata, SEMCO) plus Taiwan/China beneficiaries, and a second-order pull on AI-server BOM cost (MS "MLCC Super Cycle", 2026-06-22).

## State of play (latest)

- **"The other AI squeeze."** AI is shifting demand from semis to MLCCs, creating a supply-demand mismatch in high-end components; MS compares it to legacy DDR4 DRAM ~2 years ago, when DRAM makers redirected fixed capacity to HBM and triggered structural price hikes (MS "MLCC Super Cycle", 2026-06-22).
- **Content explosion.** A single AI server consumes ~440,000 MLCCs, 10–15x a traditional server. NVIDIA's GB300 uses ~320k units per full cabinet (~30x a high-end smartphone); a fully configured Rubin NVL72 / VR200 rack runs ~570k MLCCs (+~80% vs GB300), with total dollar content per rack up ~182%. An AI server mainboard alone carries an estimated 15,000–25,000 MLCCs (~10x a general server) (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Structural, not cyclical.** MS estimates AI-driven MLCC demand more than quadruples 2025→2030 while total capacity grows only ~10–15% p.a. Premium high-reliability / high-voltage lines are effectively booked out for multiple years; lead times exceed 20 weeks for high-end product. Greenfield capacity needs ~2 years from commitment to production and equipment is customized in-house, limiting fast efficiency gains (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Channel checks (pricing inflecting).** Distributor prices +200–300% since 1Q26; leading suppliers raised distributor/contract pricing +20–30% QoQ in 2Q26 (some removing volume discounts and refusing 1Q low-price orders); Shenzhen Huaqiangbei spot pricing up 2–10x for some specs (e.g. 0805 27µF ~6x YTD). Supply-chain inventory lean after two years of destocking (distributors ~1.5 months, downstream <1 month). MS flags some extreme 10–20x spot moves as trader hoarding, not end demand, but expects a more durable trend than the 2025 tariff spike (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Market sizing.** Global MLCC shipment value $14.7bn (2025) → $24.3bn (2028e), ~18% CAGR vs a 6.5% ten-year trend. AI-server MLCC TAM ~$900M by 2027e, could exceed $1bn by 2030e. High-cap 47µF+ MLCC demand ~4bn units (CY25) → ~38bn units (CY30); 47µF+ rises to >30% of units in Rubin-gen platforms vs <20% in GB300 (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Market structure — Japan-Korea duopoly ~85% of high-end.** Murata ~45% share (utilization 90–95%, AI server orders running ~2x capacity, leader in ultra-small 008004 form factors); **SEMCO (Samsung Electro-Mechanics, 009150.KS) ~40%** (Tianjin plant at full capacity). TDK, Taiyo Yuden and Yageo focus on high-voltage / specialized industrial; Walsin (Taiwan) and Guangdong Fenghua / Chaozhou Three-Circle / Eyang (China) serve commodity/consumer; Kyocera AVX and Vishay round out auto/industrial (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **>>> NAME TRAP: SEMCO ≠ Samsung Electronics. <<<** "SEMCO" here is **Samsung Electro-Mechanics (009150.KS)**, the components affiliate — NOT Samsung Electronics (005930.KS), the memory/foundry/handset giant covered on [../SAMSUNG.md](../SAMSUNG.md). Do not conflate the two; the MLCC call is on the Electro-Mechanics entity (MS, 2026-06-22).

## Key debates

- **Bull / where the money is:** Demand quadruples to 2030 while capacity grows ≤15%/yr; the scarce high-end parts re-rate on mix-driven ASP rather than transient spot spikes. MS prefers **SEMCO** (PT raised W920k → **W2,560,000**, ~20% upside; 26–28e EPS +20%/+71%/+84%; AI MLCC mix 18% (2026) → >50% by 2030; AI MLCC margin 34%→44% 2026→27; plus a W1.5trn (~$1bn) silicon-capacitor supply agreement for 2027–28 and glass-substrate optionality with Apple & Broadcom samples). In Taiwan, **Yageo OW** (business transformation, capacity shifting away from commodity MLCC; +379% YTD). In Japan, **Murata OW top pick** (rising AI mix); **Taiyo Yuden UW** (elevated expectations, capex-frozen ~5% capacity add limits upside). YTD: SEMCO +763%, Yageo +379%, Fenghua +355% (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Bear / what could break it:** The group has already run hard (MLCC up ~434% vs MSCI Asia Tech ~+101% YTD; group at ~9.6x NTM P/B vs 1.7x historical avg) — the call is now earnings revisions and scarcity value, not buying a cheap cyclical. Distributors see little consumer demand growth; some extreme spot moves are trader inventory hoarding in Huaqiangbei. A sudden bursting of AI demand, inventory risk, and broad consumer-electronics weakness are MS's named downside risks; standard X5R product is being precautionarily stockpiled but is not actually short (MS, 2026-06-22).
- **Timeline / inflection points:** MS expects tightness to hold into 2H26 and 2027 as capacity lags. Key indicators to monitor: (1) evolving AI specs, (2) sharp reversals in book-to-bill (Murata BB 1.36 in 4Q vs 1.12 prior; Taiyo Yuden 1.31 vs 1.08), (3) YoY pricing momentum, (4) AI-server demand, and confirmation of Rubin/VR200 MLCC content. Adjacent secular drivers: embedded MLCC into ABF substrates, silicon capacitors, and demand from compute / power (48V→800V architectures, ~120kW racks) / networking (CPO at 800G–1.6T) (MS, 2026-06-22).

**Q&A / supply-chain checks (memory + NAND, off-coverage):**
- **Nanya Tech (Taiwan)** — ~65K DRAM wafers/mo, **mostly legacy DDR4**; new fab (5A) adds **+15K (2027) / +20K (2028)**, total only ~45K, with very small wafer-starts (5–10K) by end-2027 (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23).
- **CXMT (China)** — DRAM cap **300K end-2026** (up only +20K vs end-2025, **mostly an HBM attempt**, won't ramp in volume; tech too old → low-grade consumer LPDDR5/DDR5 only); reaching ~360K by end-2027. **Sold out** (HP/Lenovo qualified, no capacity left for Dell); not a near-term US oversupply threat, with a good chunk of output earmarked for **HBM inside China** (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23; StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23). Equipment from Tokyo Seimitsu (compression molding, dicers/grinders).
- **YMTC (China)** — NAND capacity **growing substantially on domestic tools** (NAND easier than DRAM), the more concerning Chinese maker long-term — but **only ~13% QLC**, limiting it to consumer-grade; not a near-term concern (Conor + Avril, 2026-06-23).
- **Silicon Motion** — bullish on the **storage cycle running to 2028** (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).
- **Kioxia Super IO / XL Flash** — SLC-based ultra-low-latency NAND for agentic AI IO (not KV-cache): shipping **10M units this year (vs 3M last), targeting 100M next year, with NVIDIA as partner**; SanDisk's competing HPF/HBF not due until 2028 (R&D w/ Global Unichip) → "dead on arrival" if Super IO succeeds. (Covered names — see SNDK/KIOXIA pages — but the unit ramp is a new datapoint) (Redburn, 2026-06-23).
- **CPU-packaging beneficiaries (Taiwan):** **AAC** (TSMC outsourcing Vera/Venus CPU packaging), **Kinsus** (benefiting from Unimicron capacity constraints), **ASPEED** (high general-server exposure) — see cowos-packaging for detail (Redburn, 2026-06-23).
