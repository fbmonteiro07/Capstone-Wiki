# LITE — Lumentum Holdings

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-18 · sources: `E:\Wiki Felipe\LITE` (filings + transcripts) · `_equity_calls` · `_briefings\by-ticker\LITE.md`. Master index: [../INDEX.md](../INDEX.md)._

## Snapshot
Lumentum is the cleanest US large-cap expression of the AI-datacenter photonics buildout. Two reportable segments — **Cloud & Networking** (the engine: optical/photonic chips, components, modules, subsystems for hyperscalers and NEMs; ~86% of FY25 sales, of which Components ~65% of total company revenue) and **Industrial Tech** (cyclically soft, ~14% of FY25, -15% YoY) (LITE 10-K, FY25, 2025-08-19; segment shares per Citi LITE initiation, 2025-08-29; TD Cowen, 2026-03-11). What matters is the AI stack: indium-phosphide (InP) EML/CW/ultra-high-power (UHP) lasers, cloud transceivers (1.6T), optical circuit switches (OCS), and the four growth legs management keeps repeating — cloud transceivers, OCS, scale-out CPO, and scale-up CPO. The franchise pivot is real: legacy cyclical telco supplier → "foundational engine of the AI revolution" (Hurlston, Q2FY26, 2026-02-03). NVIDIA took a direct equity stake (+$2.02B to cash) on top of a take-or-pay laser supply agreement (Q3FY26, 2026-05-06). The buy-side debate is no longer demand — it's whether they can build it (execution/capacity), whether InP merchant supply eventually overshoots, and whether the multiple holds.

## Current state (latest quarter — Q3FY26, 2026-05-06)
- **Revenue $808.4M, +90% YoY**, record quarter, above guide midpoint (Hurlston/Ali, Q3FY26).
- **Non-GAAP GM 47.9%** (+540bps QoQ, +1,270bps YoY); **non-GAAP OM 32.2%** (+700bps QoQ, +2,140bps YoY); **non-GAAP EPS $2.37**. GAAP EPS $1.50 (Ali, Q3FY26). Already tracking toward the low end of LITE's 39%–42% long-term GM range, with the EML/CPO/OCS mix all accretive to corporate (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15).
- **EML supply-demand imbalance "greater than 30%"** — i.e. undershipping customer demand by ~30%, and widening even as capacity is added (Hurlston to Koontz, Q3FY26). Targeting **>50% growth in EML units by Dec-Q 2026**; record EML shipments +100% YoY. LITE holds ~50% AI EML unit share at 100G/lane and expects to hold ~50% at 200G/lane; Citi modeled AI EML sales ~$700M+ in FY26E and LITE as the key supplier to ~50% of 800G/1.6T EML-based transceivers (Citi LITE initiation, 2025-08-29). Tight EML supply drives pricing power on both legacy 100G/lane and new 200G/lane (JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **OCS:** "multiyear, multibillion-dollar purchase agreement"; backlog well past $400M, majority shipping in 2027, three customers (Q2FY26 + Q3FY26). Dec-Q calendar exit-run-rate well above the prior ~$100M peg. Citi modeled OCS at $100M+ quarterly by FY28E on the three key customers (Citi initiation, 2025-08-29). TD Cowen (Hold) frames OCS upside positively but flags that modeling beyond Google's TPU scale-up use case is highly speculative (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11).
- **CPO/scale-out:** ~$50M Dec-Q (cal Q4 '26); an incremental multi-$100M UHP order clicking in 1H'27; scale-up CPO first shipments late cal-2027 (Q2FY26). Pump-laser constraint flagged as "biggest… somewhat unanticipated" bottleneck (Hurlston to Rakesh, Q3FY26). CPO ramps "more meaningfully" from the Dec-Q this year through cal-27, then another inflection in cal-28 as Greensboro comes online; **first scale-up CPO demand (NVL576 / Feynman) starts around early cal-28** (LITE IR call, 2026-05-11). NVDA's scale-out CPO adoption now tracking **>50%** vs ~40% a few quarters ago — primary-customer CPO demand has stepped up over time (JPM "Networking" notes, 2026-05-26).
- **Scale-across / pumps — new growth leg.** "Scale across" pump-laser components today run **mid-$10s (~$40–60M/qtr)** and are guided to **3–4x output/revenue over the next 4–6 quarters**; LITE holds **70–80% of the pump-laser market and similar on ITLAs**, and is "essentially the kingmaker in terms of allocation" — pump supply is behind demand by **~50–60%**, more pronounced than EMLs, driving **mid-double-digit % price increases** on pumps (Michael Hurlston, JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07). Demand is not just Ciena/Meta — **Nokia, Cisco, Molex and Ciena** are all placing scale-across orders, splitting the back-end of the network across a DCI framework (LITE IR call, 2026-05-11). The high-margin **charge-pump line (~$30M/qtr run-rate) doubles next quarter** on scale-across demand, with gross and operating margins better than even EMLs (LITE IR call, 2026-05-11). Pumps share the Santa Fe fab with phase-one CPO, forcing Caswell UK into faster/higher-volume service for CPO (JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07).
- **Transceivers — sole-source at Google.** LITE has reached the "front of the line" at its primary transceiver customer (Google), holding **100% share on a couple of key 1.6T SKUs** near-term (more temporal than the OCS moat — Innolight/Coherent will catch up in a quarter or two); Google's new **Virgo back-end network is an incremental optics opportunity that lifts optics attach per TPU** (Hurlston/Yuen, JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07). Transceiver business expected to **roughly double over the next ~4 quarters** despite manufacturing/margin still the soft spot (JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07). LITE is feeding ~**20% of its transceiver mix with its own CW lasers** (in response to a CW-laser-diode decommit from an LD supplier), borrowing from InP capacity and modestly moderating EML revenue growth (JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07; JPM "Networking" recap, 2026-05-08).
- **OCS Sep-Q inflection quantified:** OCS ~$70M in the June quarter steps to **~$170M/quarter in 1H-FY27** (≈$100M of sequential growth from OCS alone), making the September quarter the primary revenue driver; **Dec-Q OCS ≥$200M, hopefully ~$250M** run-rate (JPM "Networking" recap, 2026-05-08; LITE IR call, 2026-05-11). Non-NVIDIA customers are now seeking to lock the **remaining Greensboro fab capacity**, pressuring NVDA to make a further fab investment for visibility (JPM "Networking" notes, 2026-05-26). ~**90%+ of fab allocation is set in take-or-pay agreements**; incremental units above committed take-or-pay are **auctioned off** at healthier pricing (JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07).
- **Q4FY26 guide:** rev **$960M–$1.01B**, non-GAAP OM **35%–36%**, non-GAAP EPS **$2.85–$3.05** on ~102M shares (Ali, Q3FY26). At $805M Q3 print they are ~halfway to the OFC target of **$2B quarterly rev / 40% OM in 18–24 months** (Hurlston @ JPM, 2026-05-18).
- **Capacity:** 5th InP fab acquired in Greensboro NC (six-inch from the start, ~2yr to HVP → 2028); 40% Sagamihara expansion front-loaded (>half delivered in Dec-Q); Caswell UK and Takao Japan being pulled into service; California fab expansion scaling UHP lasers for AI CPO (JPM, 2025-12-15). CapEx $125M, capacity-led. Cash $3.17B post-NVIDIA (Ali, Q3FY26). Datacom (EML + transceivers) modeled +98% in FY26E, +45%/+26% FY27E/FY28E by Citi (FY25→FY28 CAGR ~53%) (Citi initiation, 2025-08-29).

## Debate / thesis
- **Capstone model (house view):** revenue $1.41B→$2.11B→$4.24B→$8.14B (2024-27E; +49%/+101%/+92%) on Datacom & AI ($0.46B→$2.53B→$6.27B, ~77% of mix by 2027) incl. transceivers / OCS / CPO ramps; adj EPS $0.94 / $4.13 / $12.81 / $30.02; op margin ~4%→43%. (Capstone official model — consolidated optics, 2026-06-16)
- **Bull:**
  - **Demand isn't the question — supply is.** Hurlston: "there's no demand problem, which is an enviable situation… our issue is execution" (Samik/JPM fireside, 2026-05-18). FundaAI framed Hurlston's Mizuho remarks as "a supply problem, not demand" (briefing, 2026-06-11).
  - **Locked book, pricing power.** ~90%+ of fab allocation under take-or-pay LTAs through cal-2027; incremental units auctioned at "even healthier pricing"; LTAs killed the old quarterly price-down ritual (Hurlston/Ali, Lumentum followup call, 2026-05-07). "Sold out through 2028" possible; expects to fill the entire order book through 2028 within two quarters (MS, morning briefing 2026-05-15).
  - **Four growth legs still ahead.** "The vast majority of this growth is still ahead of us" (Hurlston, Q2FY26). Scale-up CPO is "a brand new market that didn't exist before" — >10x the InP need of scale-out (Yuen/Hurlston, Q2FY26 + JPM 2026-05-18). TD Cowen (Hold) independently sizes scale-up CPO external light sources as a **>$9B fully greenfield TAM by 2030E** (~25% port CPO penetration, ~$17/source), with LITE and COHR the key beneficiaries — and argues scale-up CPO ports rapidly eclipse scale-out (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11).
  - **NVDA validation + moat.** Jensen's three photonics investments = Lumentum, Coherent, Corning — "Not Credo. Not copper" (@Midnight_Captl, briefing 2026-05-12). $2B NVDA stake + take-or-pay; OCS Google design win (R64 OCS, in production). Hurlston: high-power laser reliability proven in subsea ("Mariana Trench") — multi-year head start vs new entrants (JPM, 2026-05-18). CPO content math favors LITE: 8 EML chips into a transceiver ≈ one UHP laser in content, but where LITE is ~50% share at the EML level it is **sole or primary source on NVDA's CPO UHP laser** — so transceiver displacement by CPO is more than offset by UHP content (Citi LITE initiation, 2025-08-29).
  - **800G+ datacom / DCI TAM.** 800G+ datacom transceiver market ~$7bn in 2025, telecom/DCI transceivers ~$1bn, both ~+40% CAGR through end of decade; LITE positioned to grow in line or faster on laser-component leadership across datacom and telecom (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15). Scale-across/DCI is an incremental ~$10bn+ TAM where LITE plays in coherent pluggables, RLS and amplifiers (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-05).
  - **Earnings power skews up.** JPM bull-case EPS in the low-teens, vs JPM base ~$11 and sell-side consensus ~$9.50; buy-side whisper has crept to low-teens on OCS, CPO and portfolio-wide pricing (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15). JPM ranks LITE **#5 in its 2026 best ideas** and **#2 in optical (COHR > LITE > CIEN > GLW > FN)**, the rank order driven by relative valuation/expectations, not fundamentals (JPM, 2025-12-15).
  - **Sell-side PTs (May '26, briefings):** R&Co Redburn (Mike Harrison) **Buy, PT $1,270**, rev 20% above consensus, 153-page launch "Copper if you can, optical because you must"; JPM (Chatterjee) **OW, PT $1,130** (FY27E $19.40 / FY28E $32, 35x C28F); Citi (Malik/Sylla) **Buy ~$1,100** (FY28E ~$30); Jefferies (Jeff Pu) **Buy $1,168**. The original Citi launch was **Buy, $165 PT** (pre-split; ~23x FY27E EPS ~$7.16, a 21% premium to ~19x AI-optics peers, justified on a 58% vs 40% EPS-CAGR basis), preferring LITE to COHR over 12 months on faster growth (FY26 rev +42%, 29% 3yr CAGR) and faster margin expansion (~850bps GM FY25→FY28; GM modeled 41.9%/43.3% FY27/FY28) (Citi LITE initiation, 2025-08-29).
- **Bear:**
  - **Execution is the whole tape.** JPM's own framing: "Lots of irons in the fire; execution the concern" (Meyers, briefing 2026-05-08). Hurlston himself admits margin/manufacturing on transceivers is the soft spot.
  - **Transceiver margin drag.** LITE transceiver GM "in the 20s," vs Coherent mid-30s and Chinese/Innolight mid-40s; line of sight to 30s, still well below the ~50% corporate level even as the business roughly doubles over 4–5 quarters (Hurlston, followup call 2026-05-07). Citi pegged the internal-SiPho transceiver GM profile at 30–35% even after bringing CW lasers fully in-house (1HC2026), with cloud-module execution historically "choppy" and the biggest near-term margin swing factor (Citi initiation, 2025-08-29).
  - **InP merchant oversupply — the structural bear.** TD Cowen initiates **LITE at Hold and prefers COHR**: it sees simultaneous InP capacity adds (LITE +40% units Sep-25→Jun-26, Sumitomo 2.4x to 2028, Mitsubishi shifting ~$1.5bn capex toward optics) colliding with a mix shift to silicon photonics / CW lasers. Because CW dies are ~60% the size of an EML and yield better, a given wafer supports **>4x (Sumitomo math ~4.5x) the transceivers** even at flat wafer starts — priming the merchant market to tip into oversupply. Risk is acute for LITE specifically because Components is ~65% of revenue (vs ~2% at Sumitomo, ~0.5% at Mitsubishi) and its cash-flow base for capex is far smaller; COHR's vertical integration lets it dial down external laser purchases, compounding LITE's exposure. TD Cowen explicitly likens the LTA "visibility through 2027" to the 2021/22 analog-semis cycle: "LTAs give visibility… until they don't" (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11). The SiPh/CW shift is also an apples-to-apples revenue headwind as cheaper CW displaces higher-ASP EML (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11).
  - **Valuation/positioning.** JPM bear tenet: limited appetite to pay ~30x earnings (vs ~20x historical) with the buy-side already pricing in more pricing power than sell-side estimates embed (JPM, 2025-12-15). BofA (Vivek Arya) **Neutral, PT $1,100** — best-in-sector growth but "upside well priced in" (CY26/27E +18%/+9%) (briefing 2026-05-06). Barclays (Rand): sentiment "a lot more mixed than a few months ago… pushback on huge EPS numbers" (briefing 2026-05-20). @ResearchQf: "most extended vs 4yr avg," 20–40% correction plausible (briefing 2026-05-13). Stock fell -8% on 2026-06-10 on CPO-timing noise.
  - **CPO timing / cannibalization.** JEF (Beavington) flagged a CPO OIO pushout to 2030-31 on interposer overheating; MS (Marshall) and JPM (Chatterjee) call CPO concerns "overblown" and buy-the-dip, but the binary timing debate is the recurring wobble (briefings 2026-06-11/12). Scale-out CPO cannibalizes pluggable transceivers (Hurlston, JPM 2026-05-18). Note TD Cowen's offsetting view: CPO is far more likely in scale-up (greenfield) than scale-out, where pluggables stay dominant on interoperability/multi-vendor/architectural-fungibility grounds — making CPO an opportunity rather than a threat (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11).
  - **Substrate/equipment/China.** China controls 70%+ of global InP output (briefing 2026-06-13); substrate "mostly under control" via Sumitomo LTA but "tighter than I'd like"; equipment (reactors, etch) now an inhibitor (Hurlston, JPM 2026-05-18).
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Broadly constructive, PTs clustered ~$1,100–$1,270; the bull case is Redburn/JPM/Citi/Jefferies/MS; the swing voters are BofA Neutral on valuation and TD Cowen Hold on InP oversupply (the cleanest bear, preferring COHR). Independent (FundaAI, Crux, Irrational Analysis) overwhelmingly long. The actionable edge: the Street agrees on near-term demand and disagrees on (1) CPO ramp timing, (2) whether merchant InP eventually overshoots as SiPh/CW penetrates, and (3) whether the multiple (~30x vs ~20x historical) has run ahead of the 2028 numbers.

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q4FY26 / FY26 print** (fiscal year-end; Street tracking the $960M–$1.01B guide). Watch the OCS Dec-Q exit rate vs the >$100M peg and whether transceiver GM steps into the 30s.
- **Scale-out CPO inflection 2H cal-2026** (~$50M Q4); **scale-up CPO first shipments late cal-2027** — the generational leg, sized by TD Cowen at >$9B greenfield by 2030E.
- **InP capacity milestones:** Sagamihara 40%+ delivery, Caswell/Takao ramp, Greensboro six-inch yield (2028 HVP), California UHP scaling. Watch for a phase-four fab announcement (Hurlston: decision needed late-'26/early-'27 to serve '29) — and conversely for the first signs of industry-wide InP overbuild flagged by TD Cowen.
- **New LTAs / pre-pay structures** beyond NVIDIA (Hurlston targeting more "before next quarter," JPM 2026-05-18); AMD actively seeking CW laser supply (briefings 2026-06-16/18).
- **ELS module entry** — ~2–2.5x content uplift vs selling bare lasers (Yuen, Q2FY26).
- Already in the rear-view but flow-relevant: **NDX-100 inclusion effective 2026-05-18** (replaced CSGP).

## Risks
- **Customer concentration:** Customer A 16.0% / Customer B 15.4% of FY25 net revenue (>10% each); transceiver demand now skewed to one sole-source customer (LITE 10-K FY25; Q2FY26).
- **Execution/manufacturing:** test-time and "boutique component" bottlenecks (gaskets, LEDs) gating OCS; six-inch InP edge-effects unproven at HVP; new-laser-recipe requals run ~6 months (followup call 2026-05-07).
- **InP merchant oversupply / SiPh-CW substitution:** simultaneous capacity adds across LITE/Sumitomo/Mitsubishi (+COHR internalizing) against a CW mix shift that multiplies effective supply (~4x+ transceivers per wafer); LITE most exposed given Components ~65% of revenue and smaller capex base; LTAs may mask the turn until it arrives (TD Cowen, 2026-03-11).
- **Supply chain:** InP substrate dependence (Sumitomo LTA), semicap equipment lead times, China geopolitical/export exposure on InP (10-K FY25 risk factors; briefings).
- **Technology inflections / commoditization:** SiPh share gain at 1.6T, Chinese entrants, CPO/NPO cannibalizing pluggables; 3.2T reverts to EML (Hurlston, JPM 2026-05-18).
- **Multiple risk:** stock +136% YTD into May; ~30x vs ~20x historical (JPM, 2025-12-15); extended technicals; CPO-timing headlines drive sharp drawdowns.
