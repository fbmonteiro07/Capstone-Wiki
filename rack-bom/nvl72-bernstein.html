CHECKHBM cost cross-check vs SemiAnalysis (Apr 26 model)

Rubin = 8× HBM4 24Gb 12-Hi stacks (288 GB/GPU, 20.7 TB/rack) in both models — the disagreement is price, not content

Where they diverge: Bernstein's 2027E HBM4 at $48/GB cost (+10% Nvidia mark-up → $53/GB in the customer BOM) is ~1.8–2.0x the SemiAnalysis Apr-26 curve, which already embeds a +40% HBM4 step-up in 1Q27 ($671 → $939/stack, i.e. $26.1/GB) and a blended HBM ASP of $22.3/GB for 2027 (+47% YoY). At the SemiAnalysis 2027 reference price the VR rack is ~$8.5M, not $9.1M. On today's price the two are close but not identical: SemiAnalysis' 2026 reference ($18.6/GB) already runs ~12% above Bernstein's $16.63 current contract. Supporting Bernstein's hawkishness: UBS (Arcuri, 21-mai-26) flags HBM4E pricing at "roughly double" HBM3E and "$9,000 worth of DRAM" inside a Vera system; memory shortage commentary has broadened (ZS capex, autos, PCs). The truth likely sits between the two curves — and both notes agree investors must re-mark this line monthly.

Source: Bernstein "AI Value Chain: How much does a GW of Vera Rubin data center capacity actually cost?" (Stacy Rasgon, Daniel Zhu et al., 8 Jun 2026), Exhibits 1, 2, 5, 6; Morgan Stanley "NV AI Server Model" (Kao/Shih, 8 Jun 26) via NVL72_BOM.html; SemiAnalysis Accelerator Model Apr-26 ("HBM Pricing" reference ASPs, "HBM Supplier summary" blended $/GB).

Key Bernstein assumptions: Rubin GPU $55K × 72 (ex-HBM); Vera CPU $5K × 36; HBM4 $16.63/GB today ($2.08/Gb × 8) rising to $48/GB cost in 2027 when VR ships in volume, +~10% Nvidia mark-up → $53/GB in customer BOM ($344K → $1.09M per rack); LPDDR5X SOCAMM at $14.85/GB (2Q26 mobile LP5X contract $11.43 +30% SOCAMM premium) × 54 TB = $802K; TLC NAND $0.37/GB (+30% vs client) × 3.5 PB (2,248 TB E1.S carried over from GB200 + 1,217 TB ICMS at 16.9 TB/GPU) = $1.28M. Networking ~13% of rack (8% scale-up / 5% scale-out). Nvidia assumed to pass HBM increases through via dynamic pricing rather than absorb in margin.

Transcription notes vs the printed report: (1) GB200 scale-up components as printed (NVLink $184K + cabling $123K + backplanes $102K = $409K) do not foot to Bernstein's $287K scale-up subtotal — VR column foots exactly; subtotals used as printed. (2) Report text says ICMS adds "16.9 GB/GPU" — Exhibit 2 (1,217 TB/rack) implies 16.9 TB/GPU. (3) Exhibit 6's "Performance per GW (FP16 Sparse EFLOPS)" row is computed as FP8 PFLOPS/rack × racks/GW (labels FP8/FP16 inconsistent in source; figures shown as printed). (4) Text cites GB200 rack at 130 kW, Exhibit 5 uses 132 kW. (5) H100 column components sum to $2,027K vs $2,026K printed (rounding).
