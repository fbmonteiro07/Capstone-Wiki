Notes

  1. Model usage: Capstone NVDA model supplies NVDA figures only (GW, revenue, units); all custom-silicon client figures (Google / Anthropic / OpenAI / META GW and AI revenue) come from the Capstone AVGO model.
  2. Basis differences: SemiAnalysis chip-implied GW = shipments × all-in chip power (IT load incl. server/networking overhead, no facility PUE), booked in shipment year. Capstone NVDA GW = deployed capacity grossed up ~1.5× (PUE 1.1 + 35% other) — closer to grid power. Capstone AVGO = client datacenter GW served by AVGO silicon.
  3. Nvidia power scope (revised): all DC accelerators in the SemiAnalysis supply model — Hopper (incl. H20/H20E), Blackwell (incl. RTX Pro 6000, B30A), Rubin, Rubin Ultra, Rubin CPX standalone (all-in power estimated at TDP × 1.431, the VR ratio — no all-in figure in the source). Excludes Groq, OpenAI Titan, Vera CPUs (rack power already allocated to GPU all-in).
  4. Vera Rubin counting: one supply-model unit = one dual-die package at 2,300 W TDP / 3,291 W all-in PER PACKAGE. Counted as 2 chips under Nvidia's NVL convention (Ultra = 4). The Capstone NVDA model was corrected on 10-Jun-26 to count Rubin dies (chips ×2, W/chip halved to 1,150 W per die — GW deployed unchanged): 13.97M chips in 2027 vs SemiAnalysis die-basis 14.95M, within ~7%.
  5. AVGO 2027: SA-implied XPU revenue ($163bn) is +82% vs Capstone ASIC ($90bn), driven by Anthropic premium system-level ASPs ($33.8–37k/chip) and the OpenAI Titan ramp ($44bn). Capstone AVGO carries OpenAI at only $13bn compute in 2027. 2026 is nearly identical ($55.4bn vs $51.7bn).
  6. Capstone NVDA model years treated as calendar (FY ~ CY+1 offset ignored); AVGO model 2025E–2027E columns used as-is.
